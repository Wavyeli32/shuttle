Muriel Bowser

Muriel Bowser has served as the Mayor of Washington, D.C., since taking office on January 2, 2015. Born on August 2, 1972, in Washington, D.C., she has been a member of the Democratic Party throughout her political career. Before becoming mayor, Bowser served on the D.C. Council, representing Ward 4 from 2007 to 2015. She succeeded Adrian Fenty in the Council position after he became mayor, highlighting her deep-rooted involvement in local politics.

Eric Adams

Born on September 1, 1960, in Brooklyn, New York, Eric Adams is a former police officer and politician. He served in the New York City Police Department for over 20 years, rising to the rank of captain. Adams later entered politics, serving as a Democratic member of the New York State Senate from 2006 to 2013. During his tenure, he focused on issues related to public safety, education, and healthcare. Adams won the mayoral election in 2021, succeeding Bill de Blasio. As mayor, he has emphasized public safety, education reform, and economic recovery for the city.
